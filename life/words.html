Words

It fills the rooms in my body,
the walls like skin in warm wool,
red west beams on a warm winter’s
Words
whirls and whisk makes dough
bakes waffles
waters the ways of words
what would this be without
the whooshing of the ocean waves
bouncing red west beams
the surfing moon slices the
waffle of water waves
the surfing moon the yeast
of the wet sand, the dough
of the shore baking and basking
in soft pillow shards
Words
cuts the core of the mother’s cake icing
the sharp western beam and the
surfing moon slaps against
basking pillow shards like
the sweet papilla slapping on
the slander of a foul mouth’s saliva.
